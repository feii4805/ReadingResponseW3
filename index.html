My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge. What could yours be?

Quote 1

"Artists excel at creating worlds. They do this first for themselves and then, when they share their work, for others. Of course, world-building means creating everything—not only making things inside the world but also the surrounding world itself—the language, style, rules, and architecture."

Quote 2

"My favorite aspect of websites is their duality: they’re both subject and object at once. In other words, a website creator becomes both author and architect simultaneously. There are endless possibilities as to what a website could be. What kind of room is a website? Or is a website more like a house? A boat? A cloud? A garden? A puddle? Whatever it is, there’s potential for a self-reflexive feedback loop: when you put energy into a website, in turn the website helps form your own identity."

Response

The idea of a website as both a room and architect designs is really interesting. It means a website isn't just a place to drop information, but it's also a space or platform that someone has thoughtfully made. For artists, who are good at creating new things, a website is a perfect place to show off their work. They can build the space just how they like to tell a story or share a feeling. The website is like a mirror to the diversity of artists, reflecting their creativity. The website provides the ability to let artists create and change both online and off.
